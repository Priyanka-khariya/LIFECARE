Gynaecological Diseases

➤Menstrual cycle-related issues, e.g., menopause (It is a part of a woman’s life where she goes 12 consecutive months without menstruating. The menopause mostly begins during the 40s), menorrhagia (heavy menstrual bleeding).
➤ Cancers of any of the parts, e.g., ovaries, breast, cervix.
➤ CVDs also account for 45% of deaths in the 40-69 year age group.
➤ Pregnancy-related concerns, e.g., abortion, miscarriage, postpartum depression, gestational diabetes.
➤Vaginal Infections: Vaginal yeast infection- This condition causes the skin around the vagina to itch and burn and may change the consistency of vaginal discharge. It is caused due to an overgrowth of the fungus candida..

Precautions

➤ Follow a healthy lifestyle. Eat right, exercise, and manage your weight
➤ Have regular check-ups with your gynecologist, i.e., once a year or more, if you have an existing condition.
➤ Use clean water to wash your genital area.
➤ Use a condom to protect yourself from sexually transmitted diseases (STDs).
➤ Do not ignore postmenopausal bleeding..
➤Incontinence and prolapse are not usually dangerous. Do pelvic floor exercises (Kegel exercises) to strengthen your pelvic muscles.

Symptoms

➤ Abnormal vaginal bleeding
➤ Bleeding between periods
➤ Urgent or frequent need to urinate
➤ Burning sensation while urinating
➤ Pain or pressure in your pelvis Increased vaginal discharge
➤ Lumps or soreness in the genital area
➤ Vaginal discharge with unusual or unpleasant odour or unusual color
➤Swelling, burning, itching, soreness, or redness in the vaginal area
➤Bleeding after menopause.
➤Breast-related symptoms such as a change in breast size, lump/mass in the breast, redness in the nipple or skin, etc.
